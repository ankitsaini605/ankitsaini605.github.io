Aptitude Topic 58: Time and Work Problems

Master Time and Work problems with these example questions and detailed solutions to help you prepare efficiently.

Problem 1:

A can complete a work in 12 days, and B can complete the same work in 16 days. If both work together, how many days will they take to finish the work?
Solution:
Work done by A in 1 day = 1/12
Work done by B in 1 day = 1/16
Work done together in 1 day = 1/12 + 1/16 = (4 + 3)/48 = 7/48
Total days to finish the work together = 1 / (7/48) = 48/7 ≈ 6.86 days.
Answer: Approximately 6.86 days

Problem 2:

A and B can do a piece of work in 15 days and 20 days respectively. They work together for 5 days. How much work is left?
Solution:
Work done by A in 1 day = 1/15
Work done by B in 1 day = 1/20
Work done together in 1 day = 1/15 + 1/20 = (4 + 3)/60 = 7/60
Work done in 5 days = 5 × 7/60 = 35/60 = 7/12
Work left = 1 - 7/12 = 5/12
Answer: 5/12 of the work is left

Problem 3:

A alone can do a work in 24 days. He works for 8 days and then B completes the remaining work in 12 days. How long will B take to do the entire work alone?
Solution:
Work done by A in 1 day = 1/24
Work done by A in 8 days = 8/24 = 1/3
Remaining work = 1 - 1/3 = 2/3
B completes 2/3 work in 12 days.
Work done by B in 1 day = (2/3) / 12 = 2 / 36 = 1/18
So, B alone can do the work in 18 days.
Answer: 18 days
