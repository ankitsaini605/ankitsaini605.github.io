Introduction

Ratio and Proportion are fundamental mathematical concepts often used in various aptitude tests. Understanding how to compare quantities and solve proportion problems is critical for competitive exams.

Key Concepts

  • Ratio: A comparison of two quantities expressed as a:b or a/b.
  • Proportion: An equation stating that two ratios are equal.
  • Cross multiplication is used to solve proportion problems.

Formulas

  • If a:b = c:d, then ad = bc.
  • Mean Proportional between a and b = √(ab).
  • If a:b = c:d, then a/c = b/d.

Practice Problems

Problem 1

The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 5:6. If there are 30 boys, how many girls are there?

Given boys:girls = 5:6 and boys = 30.
Let girls = x.
5/6 = 30/x → 5x = 180 → x = 36.
Therefore, there are 36 girls.

Problem 2

In a recipe, the ratio of flour to sugar is 3:1. If you want to make 16 cups of mixture, how much flour and sugar will you need?

Ratio flour:sugar = 3:1 → total parts = 4.
Total mixture = 16 cups.
Flour = (3/4) × 16 = 12 cups.
Sugar = (1/4) × 16 = 4 cups.

Tips & Tricks

  • Always simplify ratios before solving.
  • Use cross multiplication for quick solutions.
  • Check your answer by verifying both sides of the proportion.
