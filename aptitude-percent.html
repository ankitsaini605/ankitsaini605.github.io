Percentage – Core Concepts

What is Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. It is denoted by the symbol "%". The word percentage comes from the Latin word 'per centum' which means 'by the hundred'.

For example, 45% represents 45 out of 100, or the ratio 45:100, or the fraction 45/100.

Basic Percentage Formulas

Percentage = (Value / Total Value) × 100%
Value = (Percentage × Total Value) / 100%
Total Value = (Value × 100%) / Percentage

Example 1: Finding a Percentage

Question: What is 25% of 200?

Identify the percentage (25%) and the total value (200).
Use the formula: Value = (Percentage × Total Value) / 100%
Value = (25 × 200) / 100 = 5000 / 100 = 50
Answer: 25% of 200 is 50.

Example 2: Percentage Change

Question: A number is increased by 20% and then decreased by 20%. What is the net change?

Let's assume the original number is x.
After a 20% increase, the new value becomes: x + 20% of x = x + 0.2x = 1.2x
After a 20% decrease from 1.2x, the final value becomes: 1.2x - 20% of 1.2x = 1.2x - 0.2(1.2x) = 1.2x - 0.24x = 0.96x
The net change is 0.96x - x = -0.04x, which is a 4% decrease.
Answer: There is a net decrease of 4%.

Example 3: Finding the Original Value

Question: If 60% of students passed, how many failed out of 500?

If 60% passed, then 100% - 60% = 40% failed.
Use the formula: Value = (Percentage × Total Value) / 100%
Number of failed students = (40 × 500) / 100 = 20,000 / 100 = 200
Answer: 200 students failed.

Pro Tips for Percentage Problems

  • Fraction to Percentage: To convert a fraction to a percentage, multiply by 100%. For example, 3/4 = 3/4 × 100% = 75%.
  • Decimal to Percentage: To convert a decimal to a percentage, multiply by 100%. For example, 0.35 = 0.35 × 100% = 35%.
  • Percentage to Fraction: To convert a percentage to a fraction, divide by 100%. For example, 25% = 25/100 = 1/4.
  • Percentage to Decimal: To convert a percentage to a decimal, divide by 100%. For example, 75% = 75/100 = 0.75.

Quick Calculation Shortcuts

  • Finding 10%: Simply move the decimal point one place to the left. For example, 10% of 250 = 25.
  • Finding 5%: Find 10% and then divide by 2. For example, 5% of 250 = 10% of 250 ÷ 2 = 25 ÷ 2 = 12.5.
  • Finding 20%: Find 10% and then multiply by 2. For example, 20% of 250 = 10% of 250 × 2 = 25 × 2 = 50.
  • Finding 25%: Divide by 4. For example, 25% of 250 = 250 ÷ 4 = 62.5.
  • Finding 50%: Divide by 2. For example, 50% of 250 = 250 ÷ 2 = 125.

Real-World Applications

Percentage in Daily Life

  • Discounts and Sales: "20% off" means you pay 80% of the original price.
  • Interest Rates: Banks offer interest rates as percentages of your deposit or loan amount.
  • Tax Calculations: Income tax, sales tax, and GST are all calculated using percentages.
  • Academic Grading: Exam scores are often expressed as percentages.
  • Statistics and Data Analysis: Percentages are used to represent proportions in data.

Example: Discount Calculation

Question: A shirt is priced at ₹1,200 and is on sale for 15% off. What is the sale price?

Calculate the discount amount: 15% of ₹1,200 = (15 × 1,200) / 100 = ₹180
Subtract the discount from the original price: ₹1,200 - ₹180 = ₹1,020
Answer: The sale price is ₹1,020.

Example: Simple Interest

Question: If you deposit ₹10,000 in a bank that offers 6% annual simple interest, how much interest will you earn after 2 years?

Use the simple interest formula: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time
Interest = ₹10,000 × 6% × 2 = ₹10,000 × 0.06 × 2 = ₹1,200
Answer: You will earn ₹1,200 in interest after 2 years.

1. If the price of a book is first increased by 10% and then reduced by 10%, the net effect on the price is:

No change in price
1% decrease in price
1% increase in price
2% decrease in price

Explanation: Let's say the original price is x.

After 10% increase: x + 10% of x = x + 0.1x = 1.1x

After 10% decrease from the new price: 1.1x - 10% of 1.1x = 1.1x - 0.11x = 0.99x

The final price is 0.99x, which is 1% less than the original price x.

2. In an examination, 80% of the students passed in English, 85% passed in Mathematics, and 75% passed in both subjects. What percentage of students failed in both subjects?

15%
20%
10%
5%

Explanation: Let's use the formula: P(A ∪ B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A ∩ B)

Where P(A) = percentage passed in English = 80%

P(B) = percentage passed in Mathematics = 85%

P(A ∩ B) = percentage passed in both = 75%

P(A ∪ B) = percentage passed in at least one subject = 80% + 85% - 75% = 90%

Therefore, percentage failed in both subjects = 100% - 90% = 10%

3. A man spends 30% of his income on food, 20% on rent, and 10% on travel. What percentage of his income is left for other expenses?

30%
40%
50%
60%

Explanation: Total percentage spent = 30% + 20% + 10% = 60%

Percentage left for other expenses = 100% - 60% = 40%

4. If the population of a town increases by 5% annually, by what percentage will it increase after 2 years?

10%
10.25%
10.5%
11%

Explanation: Let's say the initial population is P.

After 1 year: P + 5% of P = P + 0.05P = 1.05P

After 2 years: 1.05P + 5% of 1.05P = 1.05P + 0.05(1.05P) = 1.05P + 0.0525P = 1.1025P

The percentage increase after 2 years = (1.1025P - P) / P × 100% = 0.1025 × 100% = 10.25%

5. A shopkeeper marks his goods 25% above the cost price and gives a discount of 10% on the marked price. What is his profit percentage?

10%
12.5%
12.5%
15%

Explanation: Let's say the cost price is ₹100.

Marked price = ₹100 + 25% of ₹100 = ₹100 + ₹25 = ₹125

Discount = 10% of ₹125 = ₹12.5

Selling price = ₹125 - ₹12.5 = ₹112.5

Profit = ₹112.5 - ₹100 = ₹12.5

Profit percentage = (₹12.5 / ₹100) × 100% = 12.5%

Additional Practice Problems

Question 1: In a class of 50 students, 40% are girls. How many boys are there in the class?

Solution:

Number of girls = 40% of 50 = (40 × 50) / 100 = 20

Number of boys = 50 - 20 = 30

Answer: There are 30 boys in the class.

Question 2: A man's salary increases by 10% every year. If his starting salary is ₹20,000, what will be his salary after 3 years?

Solution:

Starting salary = ₹20,000

After 1 year: ₹20,000 + 10% of ₹20,000 = ₹20,000 + ₹2,000 = ₹22,000

After 2 years: ₹22,000 + 10% of ₹22,000 = ₹22,000 + ₹2,200 = ₹24,200

After 3 years: ₹24,200 + 10% of ₹24,200 = ₹24,200 + ₹2,420 = ₹26,620

Answer: His salary after 3 years will be ₹26,620.

Question 3: A shopkeeper bought an article for ₹800 and sold it at a profit of 25%. What is the selling price?

Solution:

Cost price = ₹800

Profit = 25% of ₹800 = (25 × 800) / 100 = ₹200

Selling price = Cost price + Profit = ₹800 + ₹200 = ₹1,000

Answer: The selling price is ₹1,000.
