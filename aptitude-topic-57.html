Problem 1:

The ratio of the ages of A and B is 5:7. After 6 years, the ratio will become 3:4. What is the present age of A?
Solution:
Let the present ages of A and B be 5x and 7x respectively.
After 6 years, their ages will be (5x + 6) and (7x + 6).
According to the problem:
(5x + 6) / (7x + 6) = 3 / 4
Cross-multiply:
4(5x + 6) = 3(7x + 6)
20x + 24 = 21x + 18
21x - 20x = 24 - 18
x = 6
Present age of A = 5x = 5 * 6 = 30 years.
Answer: 30 years

Problem 2:

Two numbers are in the ratio 4:5. If the sum of the numbers is 180, find the numbers.
Solution:
Let the numbers be 4x and 5x.
Sum = 4x + 5x = 9x = 180
x = 180 / 9 = 20
Numbers are 4x = 80 and 5x = 100.
Answer: 80 and 100

Problem 3:

The ratio of the quantities of two liquids in a mixture is 3:7. If 5 liters of the mixture is replaced by pure liquid B, the new ratio becomes 3:8. Find the quantity of the mixture.
Solution:
Let total quantity of mixture = x liters.
Initially, quantity of A = (3/10)x, quantity of B = (7/10)x.
5 liters mixture replaced, so 5 liters removed in the ratio 3:7, and 5 liters pure B added.
New quantity of A = (3/10)x - (3/10)*5 = (3/10)(x - 5)
New quantity of B = (7/10)x - (7/10)*5 + 5 = (7/10)(x - 5) + 5
New ratio = 3:8
So:
[(3/10)(x - 5)] / [(7/10)(x - 5) + 5] = 3/8
Cross multiply:
8 * (3/10)(x - 5) = 3 * [(7/10)(x - 5) + 5]
(24/10)(x - 5) = 3 * [(7/10)(x - 5) + 5]
(12/5)(x - 5) = 3 * [(7/10)(x - 5) + 5]
(12/5)(x - 5) = (21/10)(x - 5) + 15
Multiply both sides by 10:
24(x - 5) = 21(x - 5) + 150
24x - 120 = 21x - 105 + 150
24x - 120 = 21x + 45
24x - 21x = 45 + 120
3x = 165
x = 55 liters.
Answer: 55 liters
