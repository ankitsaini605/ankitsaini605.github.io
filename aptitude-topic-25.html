Introduction

Compound Interest (CI) is the interest calculated on the principal amount and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.

Key Concepts

  • Principal (P): The initial amount invested or loaned.
  • Rate (R): Annual interest rate (in percentage).
  • Time (T): Number of years the money is invested or borrowed.
  • Compound Interest (CI): Interest earned on both principal and accumulated interest.
  • Amount (A): Total sum after interest = Principal + Compound Interest.

Formulas

  • Amount (A) = P × (1 + R/100)T
  • Compound Interest (CI) = A - P
  • When compounded half-yearly: A = P × (1 + R/200)2T
  • When compounded quarterly: A = P × (1 + R/400)4T

Practice Problems

Problem 1

If ₹10,000 is invested at 8% per annum compounded annually, what will be the amount after 3 years?

A = 10000 × (1 + 8/100)3 = 10000 × (1.08)3 = ₹12597.92

Compound Interest = 12597.92 - 10000 = ₹2597.92

Problem 2

Find the compound interest on ₹15,000 at 10% per annum compounded half-yearly for 2 years.

Rate per half-year = 10/2 = 5%

Number of periods = 2 × 2 = 4

A = 15000 × (1 + 5/100)4 = 15000 × (1.05)4 = ₹18289.06

Compound Interest = 18289.06 - 15000 = ₹3289.06

Tips & Tricks

  • Convert the rate and time according to the compounding frequency.
  • Remember CI is always more than SI for the same rate and time.
  • Use approximation techniques for faster calculations.
