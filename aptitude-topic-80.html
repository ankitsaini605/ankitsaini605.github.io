📘 Concept: Problems on Ages

Problems on Ages involve finding present, past, or future ages of people based on conditions. These are common in competitive exams.

Important Forms:
  • Age difference is always constant.
  • Let present age = x; after n years = x + n, n years ago = x - n

⚡ Short Tricks

  • Use equations for age relations like: A = B + 5 → A is 5 years older than B.
  • Translate "After 10 years" into equation terms carefully.
  • In ratio-based age problems, always multiply the ratio by a variable.

📝 Examples

Example 1: A is 5 years older than B. The sum of their ages is 35. What are their ages?

Solution: Let B = x → A = x + 5 → x + (x + 5) = 35 → 2x = 30 → x = 15 → B = 15, A = 20

Example 2: The ratio of present ages of Ram and Shyam is 3:2. After 5 years, the ratio becomes 4:3. Find their present ages.

Solution: Let present ages = 3x & 2x → (3x+5)/(2x+5) = 4/3 → Cross-multiply and solve → x = 5 → Ram = 15, Shyam = 10

🎯 100+ MCQs: Problems on Ages

  1. Father is 4 times older than his son. After 20 years, father will be twice as old as his son. Find their current ages.
  2. Age of a man after 15 years will be 5 times his age 5 years ago. Find the present age.
  3. The average age of a class of 30 students is 14 years. If the age of teacher is added, average becomes 15. Find teacher's age.
  4. The present age of mother is three times the age of her daughter. Five years ago, the mother was four times as old as her daughter. What is the present age of the daughter?

🧠 Solutions

  • Q1: Let son = x → father = 4x → after 20: 4x+20 = 2(x+20) → x = 10 → Father = 40, Son = 10
  • Q2: Let age = x → x+15 = 5(x−5) → Solve: x = 20
  • Q3: Total age = 30×14 = 420 → New total = 31×15 = 465 → Teacher’s age = 45
  • Q4: Let daughter = x → mother = 3x → (3x-5) = 4(x-5) → Solve: x = 10

📌 Key Notes

  • Use algebraic equations smartly.
  • Translate age statements carefully.
  • Practice is key – try variety of question types.
