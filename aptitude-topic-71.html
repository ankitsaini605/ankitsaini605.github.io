Topic 71: Time and Distance

This topic includes problems based on calculating distance, speed, and time, which are frequently asked in competitive exams.

Practice Questions

  1. Q1. A person covers 120 km in 3 hours. What is his speed?
  2. Q2. If a car runs at 60 km/hr, how much distance will it cover in 4 hours?
  3. Q3. A train travels 90 km in 1.5 hours. What is the average speed of the train?

Answers & Explanations

  • A1. Speed = 120 ÷ 3 = 40 km/h
  • A2. Distance = 60 × 4 = 240 km
  • A3. Speed = 90 ÷ 1.5 = 60 km/h

Expert Tip

Always use the standard formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time. Keep units consistent.
