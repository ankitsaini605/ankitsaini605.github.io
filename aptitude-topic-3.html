Introduction

Ratio and Proportion are fundamental topics in quantitative aptitude. Understanding these concepts is essential for solving a wide range of mathematical problems in competitive exams.

Key Concepts

  • Ratio: The comparison of two quantities by division.
  • Proportion: When two ratios are equal, they are said to be in proportion.

Important Formulas

  • Ratio = a : b = a/b
  • Proportion: a/b = c/d
  • Mean proportional between a and b = √(ab)
  • Continued proportion: If a : b = b : c, then b² = ac

Practice Problems

Problem 1

The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:4. If there are 21 boys, how many girls are there?

Ratio of boys:girls = 3:4

Boys = 21

Girls = (4/3) × 21 = 28

Problem 2

If a : b = 5 : 6 and b : c = 4 : 7, find the ratio a : b : c.

b is common, so multiply ratios accordingly.

a : b = 5 : 6 = 20 : 24

b : c = 4 : 7 = 24 : 42

So, a : b : c = 20 : 24 : 42

Tips & Tricks

  • Always simplify ratios to their lowest terms.
  • Use cross-multiplication for proportion problems.
  • Practice with real-life scenarios for better understanding.
