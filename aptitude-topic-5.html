Time and Work

Time and Work problems are an important part of competitive exams. This topic evaluates your ability to calculate efficiency, completion time, and collaborative work speed.

🔹 Basic Concepts:

  • If A can do a piece of work in n days, then A’s 1 day work = 1/n
  • If A is x times as efficient as B, then time taken by A is 1/x of time taken by B
  • If A and B can do a work in x and y days respectively, then working together, they can do the work in xy / (x + y) days.

🔹 Formulas:

  • Total Work = Time × Efficiency
  • Work Done = Rate × Time
  • Combined Work Rate = Sum of individual work rates

🔹 Examples:

Example 1: A can complete a job in 12 days and B in 18 days. How long will they take working together?

Solution: A’s 1-day work = 1/12, B’s = 1/18. Together = (1/12 + 1/18) = (3 + 2)/36 = 5/36.
So, total days = 36/5 = 7.2 days

Example 2: A is twice as efficient as B. Together they finish a task in 12 days. How long will A take alone?

Solution: Let B's 1-day work = x, then A’s = 2x.
Total = x + 2x = 3x = 1/12 → x = 1/36
So, A’s 1-day work = 2/36 → A alone will take 18 days.

🔹 Practice Questions:

  1. A can finish a task in 15 days and B in 20 days. Working together, how many days will it take?
  2. If A can do a piece of work in 8 days and B in 12 days, how long will they take if they work on alternate days starting with A?
  3. Three men or five women can complete a job in 12 days. How many days will 6 men and 5 women take together?

🎯 Tips to Solve Quickly:

  • Convert everything into 1-day work for easier calculations
  • Use LCM method if work units are given
  • Practice with both efficiency and time-based approaches
