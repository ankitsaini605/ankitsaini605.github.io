Introduction to Pipes and Cisterns

This topic deals with problems related to filling or emptying tanks with the help of pipes. Pipes can be inlet (filling) or outlet (emptying).

Formulas to Remember

  • Inlet pipe work = positive work
  • Outlet pipe work = negative work
  • If a pipe fills a tank in X hours, then its 1 hour work = 1/X
  • If a pipe empties a tank in Y hours, then its 1 hour work = -1/Y

Practice Problems

Problem 1

A pipe can fill a tank in 6 hours, and another can empty it in 8 hours. If both are opened together, how long will it take to fill the tank?

Filling pipe's 1 hr work = 1/6
Emptying pipe's 1 hr work = -1/8
Combined 1 hr work = (1/6 - 1/8) = (4-3)/24 = 1/24
Time taken = 24 hours.

Problem 2

Two pipes can fill a tank in 10 hours and 12 hours respectively. How long will it take if both are opened together?

First pipe's 1 hr work = 1/10
Second pipe's 1 hr work = 1/12
Combined 1 hr work = (6+5)/60 = 11/60
Time taken = 60/11 ≈ 5.45 hours (~5 hours 27 minutes).

Tips & Tricks

  • Identify inlet and outlet pipes carefully.
  • Convert every operation into work done per hour.
  • Practice different scenarios — alternate pipes, delayed opening, part filling, etc.
