🎯 Profit & Loss - Key Concepts

Profit & Loss is an important topic in competitive exams. It involves calculating profit percentage, loss percentage, cost price, selling price, and discount.

👉 Basic Formulas:

  • Profit = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)
  • Loss = Cost Price (CP) - Selling Price (SP)
  • Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
  • Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
  • SP = (100 + Profit %) / 100 × CP
  • SP = (100 - Loss %) / 100 × CP

📝 Tips & Tricks

  • When multiple articles are sold at different profit or loss %, use weighted average.
  • Successive discounts or successive gains are not added directly. Use formula: Final % = x + y + (xy/100).
  • Watch out for scenarios where marked price and discount % are given — practice well!

🧮 Practice Problems

  1. A shopkeeper gains 20% by selling an article for ₹240. Find the cost price.
  2. An article costing ₹500 is sold at a loss of 10%. Find the selling price.
  3. A person sells 2 articles at ₹500 each. On one, he gains 25%; on the other, he loses 25%. Find the overall % gain or loss.
  4. An article marked at ₹1200 is sold at 20% discount. If the shopkeeper still gains 10%, find the cost price.
