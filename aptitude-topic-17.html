Aptitude Topic 17 - Percentage

Percentage is one of the most important topics in quantitative aptitude. It forms the base of many other topics like profit and loss, simple interest, compound interest, data interpretation, and more.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Percentage = (Value / Total Value) × 100
  • X% of Y = (X / 100) × Y
  • Percentage Change = [(New Value - Old Value) / Old Value] × 100
  • Converting fraction to percentage: Multiply by 100
  • Converting percentage to fraction: Divide by 100

Common Conversions

  • 1/2 = 50%
  • 1/3 ≈ 33.33%
  • 1/4 = 25%
  • 1/5 = 20%
  • 1/8 = 12.5%
  • 1/10 = 10%

Example Problem

Question: What is 20% of 250?

Solution: (20/100) × 250 = 50

Tips & Tricks

  • Remember common percentage-to-fraction conversions.
  • Use approximation when dealing with large data sets.
  • Practice mental calculations to save time in exams.

Practice Questions

  1. Find 35% of 600.
  2. If a product’s price increases from ₹500 to ₹575, what is the percentage increase?
  3. If 70% of a number is 210, find the number.
