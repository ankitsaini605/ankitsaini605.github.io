🎯 Mixtures & Alligation - Concepts

Mixtures and Alligation is an important topic in Quantitative Aptitude, especially useful for exams like SSC, Banking, Railways, CAT, etc. It deals with calculating ratios when two or more ingredients are mixed together.

👉 Formula:

Alligation Rule Formula:
C.P. of Mean Quantity = (C1 * Q1 + C2 * Q2) / (Q1 + Q2)

Where:
C1, C2 = Cost Price or Concentration of two quantities
Q1, Q2 = Quantity of the two mixtures

📝 Tips & Tricks

  • Understand ratio and proportion concepts clearly.
  • Practice problems involving replacement and repeated replacement.
  • Visualize problems using the alligation method.
  • Know when to use the alligation shortcut vs. detailed method.

🧮 Practice Problems

  1. A container contains 40 litres of milk. 8 litres of milk is taken out and replaced with water. The process is repeated twice. What is the amount of milk left after the operations?
  2. In what ratio must water be mixed with milk costing Rs. 12 per litre so that the mixture is worth Rs. 8 per litre?
  3. In a 60-litre mixture of milk and water, the ratio of milk to water is 2:1. How much water should be added to make the ratio 1:2?
  4. A shopkeeper mixes two varieties of tea costing Rs. 300/kg and Rs. 500/kg. In what ratio should he mix them to make a blend worth Rs. 400/kg?
