Mixtures and Alligation

Mixtures and Alligation is an important topic in aptitude exams. It deals with the mixing of two or more ingredients to obtain a mixture with desired properties.

Basic Formula:

The rule of alligation helps us to find the ratio in which two or more ingredients at different prices are mixed to produce a mixture at a given price.

Examples:

  1. Example 1: A container contains 40 liters of milk. From this container, 4 liters of milk was taken out and replaced by water. This process was repeated 3 more times. Find the quantity of milk left in the container after 4 operations.
    Solution: Use the formula: Final Quantity of Milk = Initial Quantity × (1 - quantity_removed/total_quantity)^n
  2. Example 2: How much water must be added to 60 liters of milk at 15 per liter so that the cost of mixture becomes 12 per liter?
    Solution: Apply the rule of alligation.
