Overview

The Time and Work topic deals with problems where individuals or groups complete a task at different rates. These problems are common in various competitive exams and help enhance logical and quantitative reasoning skills.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Work done = Rate × Time
  • If A completes a work in x hours, then A’s 1 hour work = 1/x
  • Combined work rate of A and B = Sum of their individual rates
  • Time taken to complete work = 1 / (combined work rate)

Practice Problems

Problem 1

A can do a piece of work in 12 days, and B can do it in 15 days. How long will they take to complete the work if they work together?

A’s 1 day work = 1/12
B’s 1 day work = 1/15
Combined 1 day work = 1/12 + 1/15 = (5 + 4) / 60 = 9/60 = 3/20
Time taken = 20/3 = 6⅔ days ≈ 6 days 8 hours.

Problem 2

If A can do a work in 8 days, B in 10 days, and C in 12 days, how long will they take to complete it working together?

A’s 1 day work = 1/8
B’s 1 day work = 1/10
C’s 1 day work = 1/12
Combined 1 day work = (15 + 12 + 10) / 120 = 37/120
Time taken = 120/37 ≈ 3.24 days.

Tips & Tricks

  • Always convert all times to same units (hours or days) before solving.
  • Break down combined work problems into individual work rates.
  • Practice word problems to understand real-life applications.
