Introduction to Work and Time

Work and Time is an important topic in aptitude exams where you solve problems related to efficiency, time taken to complete a task, combined work, etc.

Formulas to Remember

  • Work = Rate × Time
  • Rate = Work / Time
  • If A can do a piece of work in X days, then A’s 1 day work = 1/X
  • If A is m times as efficient as B, then ratio of times taken = 1:m

Practice Problems

Problem 1

A can do a work in 10 days and B can do the same work in 15 days. How long will they take if they work together?

A’s 1 day work = 1/10, B’s 1 day work = 1/15
Combined 1 day work = (1/10 + 1/15) = (3+2)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
Time taken = 6 days.

Problem 2

Two pipes can fill a tank in 12 hours and 16 hours respectively. How long will it take to fill the tank if both are opened together?

1st pipe's 1 hour work = 1/12, 2nd pipe's 1 hour work = 1/16
Combined 1 hour work = (1/12 + 1/16) = (4+3)/48 = 7/48
Time taken = 48/7 ≈ 6.86 hours (~6 hours 52 minutes).

Tips & Tricks

  • Always convert given information into "work per day" format.
  • Be careful with efficiency comparisons and negative work (in pipes & cisterns).
  • Practice different types of problems like combined work, alternate days, part of work, etc.
