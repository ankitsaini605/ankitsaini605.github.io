Aptitude Topic 6: Time and Work Problems

Practice key Time and Work problems with detailed solutions to strengthen your aptitude skills for competitive exams.

Problem 1:

A can complete a work in 12 days and B in 15 days. How long will they take to complete the work together?
Solution:
Work done by A in one day = 1/12
Work done by B in one day = 1/15
Combined work in one day = 1/12 + 1/15 = (5 + 4) / 60 = 9/60 = 3/20
Time taken together = 1 / (3/20) = 20/3 = 6.67 days
Answer: 6⅔ days or 6.67 days

Problem 2:

A and B together can do a piece of work in 10 days. B alone can do it in 15 days. How long will A alone take to do the work?
Solution:
Work done by B in one day = 1/15
Work done by A and B together in one day = 1/10
Work done by A alone in one day = 1/10 - 1/15 = (3 - 2) / 30 = 1/30
Time taken by A alone = 30 days
Answer: 30 days

Problem 3:

If 5 men can complete a work in 12 days, how many men will be required to complete the same work in 8 days?
Solution:
Total work = 5 men × 12 days = 60 man-days
Number of men required for 8 days = Total work / 8 = 60 / 8 = 7.5 men
Since men cannot be fractional, at least 8 men are required.
Answer: 8 men
