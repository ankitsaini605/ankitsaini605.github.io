Problem 1:

Two pipes A and B can fill a tank in 12 hours and 15 hours respectively. Both pipes are opened together but pipe A is closed after 4 hours. How long will pipe B take to fill the remaining tank?
Solution:
Pipe A fills 1/12 of the tank per hour.
Pipe B fills 1/15 of the tank per hour.
In 4 hours, A fills: 4 × (1/12) = 1/3 of the tank.
In 4 hours, B fills: 4 × (1/15) = 4/15 of the tank.
Total filled in 4 hours = 1/3 + 4/15 = 5/15 + 4/15 = 9/15 = 3/5 of the tank.
Remaining tank = 1 - 3/5 = 2/5.
Pipe B alone fills 1/15 per hour.
Time taken by B to fill remaining = (2/5) ÷ (1/15) = (2/5) × 15 = 6 hours.
Answer: 6 hours

Problem 2:

Find the LCM and HCF of 72 and 120, and verify that their product equals the product of the two numbers.
Solution:
Prime factors:
72 = 2³ × 3²
120 = 2³ × 3 × 5
HCF = product of minimum powers = 2³ × 3 = 8 × 3 = 24
LCM = product of maximum powers = 2³ × 3² × 5 = 8 × 9 × 5 = 360
Verify: 72 × 120 = 8640
HCF × LCM = 24 × 360 = 8640
Answer: LCM = 360, HCF = 24, verified successfully

Problem 3:

A shopkeeper marks his goods 25% above cost price and allows a discount of 10%. Find his gain percent.
Solution:
Let cost price = 100
Marked price = 125
Selling price after 10% discount = 125 - 12.5 = 112.5
Gain = Selling price - Cost price = 112.5 - 100 = 12.5
Gain % = (12.5 / 100) × 100 = 12.5%
Answer: 12.5% gain
