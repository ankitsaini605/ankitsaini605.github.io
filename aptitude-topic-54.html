Aptitude Topic 54: Time and Work Problems with Solutions

Explore detailed time and work aptitude problems with step-by-step solutions to help you master this important topic for competitive exams.

Problem 1:

A can complete a work in 18 days and B in 24 days. They work together for 6 days. How much work is left?
Solution:
A's one day work = 1/18
B's one day work = 1/24
Combined one day work = 1/18 + 1/24 = (4 + 3)/72 = 7/72
Work done in 6 days = 6 × 7/72 = 42/72 = 7/12
Remaining work = 1 - 7/12 = 5/12
Answer: 5/12 of the work is left

Problem 2:

If A can do a piece of work in 10 days and B in 15 days, working together how long will they take to finish the work?
Solution:
A's one day work = 1/10
B's one day work = 1/15
Combined one day work = 1/10 + 1/15 = (3 + 2)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
Time taken together = 1 ÷ (1/6) = 6 days
Answer: 6 days

Problem 3:

Three pipes A, B, and C can fill a tank in 20, 30, and 60 minutes respectively. How long will it take to fill the tank if all three pipes work together?
Solution:
A's one minute work = 1/20
B's one minute work = 1/30
C's one minute work = 1/60
Combined one minute work = 1/20 + 1/30 + 1/60 = (3 + 2 + 1)/60 = 6/60 = 1/10
Time taken to fill tank = 1 ÷ (1/10) = 10 minutes
Answer: 10 minutes
