Topic 74: Problems on Trains

This section includes train-related speed problems often asked in competitive exams. Focus on relative speed, crossing time, and platform lengths.

Practice Questions

  1. Q1. A train 120 meters long crosses a pole in 6 seconds. What is its speed in km/h?
  2. Q2. A 200-meter train crosses a platform in 20 seconds. If the speed is 54 km/h, find the platform's length.
  3. Q3. Two trains 100m and 200m long run at 30 km/h and 60 km/h in the same direction. How long will the faster train take to cross the slower one?

Answers & Explanations

  • A1. Speed = Distance / Time = 120 / 6 = 20 m/s = 20 × 18/5 = 72 km/h
  • A2. Speed = 54 km/h = 15 m/s → Total distance = 15 × 20 = 300 m → Platform = 300 - 200 = 100 m
  • A3. Relative speed = 60 - 30 = 30 km/h = 8.33 m/s → Total distance = 100 + 200 = 300 m → Time = 300 / 8.33 ≈ 36 sec

Expert Tip

Always convert speed to m/s using the factor (5/18) or (18/5) depending on direction of conversion.
