Concept: Averages

The average (arithmetic mean) is the sum of values divided by the number of values. Averages are used widely in daily calculations like test scores, income, etc.

Formula:
  • Average = (Sum of Observations) / (Number of Observations)
  • If average of n numbers is A, total sum = n × A

Quick Tricks

  • Trick 1: When one new value is added/removed from group, adjust average accordingly.
  • Trick 2: Use deviation method for quick calculation.

Examples

Example 1: The average of 5 numbers is 20. What is their sum?

Solution: Sum = 5 × 20 = 100

Example 2: The average marks of 4 students is 50. If one new student scores 70, what is the new average?

Solution: New sum = (4 × 50) + 70 = 270, New average = 270 / 5 = 54

Top 100+ MCQs on Averages

  1. If the average of 10 numbers is 15, what is the total sum?
  2. The average of 6 consecutive even numbers is 20. Find the largest number.
  3. Average of 5 numbers is 40. Four of them are 35, 45, 50, and 30. Find the 5th number.
  4. If the average of A, B, and C is 50 and A = 40, C = 60, what is B?

Solutions

  • Q1: 10 × 15 = 150
  • Q2: Numbers = 16, 18, 20, 22, 24, 26 → Largest = 26
  • Q3: Total = 5×40 = 200 → 5th number = 200 - (35+45+50+30) = 40
  • Q4: (A + B + C)/3 = 50 → (40 + B + 60)/3 = 50 → B = 50

Key Notes

  • Average is a basic concept used across many competitive exams.
  • Memorize deviation tricks for faster calculations.
  • Practice identifying patterns in number series related to averages.
