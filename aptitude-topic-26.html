Introduction

Time and Work problems deal with how much time a person or a group takes to complete a job, or how much work is done in a certain period. These are essential for competitive exams and help in logical reasoning.

Key Concepts

  • Work: The total job to be done, often considered as 1 unit.
  • Efficiency: Amount of work done per unit time.
  • Time: Duration required to complete the work.
  • Relation: Work = Efficiency × Time

Formulas

  • If A can do a work in x days, then A’s 1 day work = 1/x
  • If A’s 1 day work = a and B’s 1 day work = b, then (A + B)’s 1 day work = a + b
  • Time taken by A and B working together = 1/(a + b)
  • Efficiency ∝ Work done per day

Practice Problems

Problem 1

A can complete a work in 12 days and B in 15 days. How long will they take to complete the work working together?

A's 1 day work = 1/12

B's 1 day work = 1/15

Together 1 day work = 1/12 + 1/15 = (5 + 4)/60 = 9/60 = 3/20

Time taken together = 1 / (3/20) = 20/3 = 6⅔ days

Problem 2

A is twice as efficient as B. If A can do a work in 10 days, then in how many days can B do the same work?

A's efficiency = 2 × B's efficiency

Time taken by A = 10 days, so A's 1 day work = 1/10

B's 1 day work = (1/10) / 2 = 1/20

So, B will take 20 days to complete the work.

Tips & Tricks

  • Always convert work to 1 unit for ease of calculation.
  • Use the formula: Work = Efficiency × Time.
  • Remember to add efficiencies when people work together.
  • Keep units consistent when calculating.
