Aptitude Topic 18 - Profit and Loss

Profit and Loss is a key topic in aptitude tests. Questions on this topic assess your ability to calculate profit percentage, loss percentage, and mark-up prices quickly and accurately.

Key Formulas

  • Profit = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)
  • Loss = Cost Price (CP) - Selling Price (SP)
  • Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
  • Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
  • SP = CP × (1 + Profit %/100)
  • SP = CP × (1 - Loss %/100)

Example Problem

Question: If the cost price of a product is ₹800 and it is sold at a profit of 25%, find the selling price.

Solution: SP = 800 × (1 + 25/100) = ₹1000

Tips & Tricks

  • Always compare profit or loss with the cost price (CP), not the selling price.
  • Practice mental calculation of percentages for speed.
  • Understand the relationship between mark-up and profit % clearly.

Practice Questions

  1. A product is sold at ₹960 with a loss of 20%. What is the cost price?
  2. If a trader gains 15% after allowing a discount of 10%, find the mark-up percentage.
  3. A person bought an article for ₹1500 and sold it for ₹1800. Find the profit %.
