Aptitude Topic 121 - Simple and Compound Interest

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 121. This section covers one of the most frequently asked topics — Simple and Compound Interest (S.I. and C.I.) in aptitude exams.

Basic Formulas

  • Simple Interest (S.I.): \( SI = \frac{P \times R \times T}{100} \)
  • Compound Interest (C.I.): \( CI = P \left(1 + \frac{R}{100}\right)^T - P \)
  • Where:
    P = Principal,
    R = Rate of Interest per annum,
    T = Time in years.

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

Find the Simple Interest on Rs. 5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.

Solution:

SI = (5000 × 8 × 3) / 100 = Rs. 1200.

Question 2:

Find the Compound Interest on Rs. 10000 at 10% per annum for 2 years.

Solution:

CI = 10000 × (1 + 0.1)2 - 10000 = 10000 × 1.21 - 10000 = Rs. 2100.

Question 3:

What will be the amount on Rs. 15000 at 12% C.I. for 3 years?

Solution:

Amount = 15000 × (1 + 0.12)3 ≈ 15000 × 1.4049 = Rs. 21073.50.

Compound Interest = Rs. 21073.50 - Rs. 15000 = Rs. 6073.50.

Tips and Tricks

  • Memorize the basic formulas for S.I. and C.I.
  • Understand the concept of compounding: yearly, half-yearly, quarterly.
  • For quick C.I. calculation for 2 or 3 years, use successive percentage formula.
  • Practice with a variety of interest-related problems to improve accuracy and speed.
