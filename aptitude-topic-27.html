Introduction

Profit and Loss questions form a major part of competitive exams. These problems involve understanding cost price, selling price, profit, and loss along with their percentages.

Key Concepts

  • Cost Price (CP): The price at which an article is purchased.
  • Selling Price (SP): The price at which an article is sold.
  • Profit: When SP > CP, Profit = SP - CP.
  • Loss: When CP > SP, Loss = CP - SP.
  • Profit Percentage: (Profit / CP) × 100%
  • Loss Percentage: (Loss / CP) × 100%

Formulas

  • Profit = SP - CP
  • Loss = CP - SP
  • Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
  • Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
  • SP = CP + Profit
  • SP = CP - Loss

Practice Problems

Problem 1

A trader buys an article for ₹500 and sells it for ₹600. Find the profit percentage.

Profit = 600 - 500 = ₹100

Profit % = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%

Problem 2

A shopkeeper sells an article at a loss of 12%. If the cost price of the article is ₹1500, find the selling price.

Loss = 12% of 1500 = 0.12 × 1500 = ₹180

Selling Price = Cost Price - Loss = 1500 - 180 = ₹1320

Tips & Tricks

  • Always remember the basic formulas.
  • Convert percentages into decimals when calculating.
  • Check if it's a profit or loss scenario before applying formulas.
