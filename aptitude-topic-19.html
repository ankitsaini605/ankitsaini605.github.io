Aptitude Topic 19 - Simple Interest

Simple Interest is a commonly asked topic in banking exams, government exams, and placement tests. It is a method of calculating interest on a principal amount over a period of time.

Formula for Simple Interest

Simple Interest (SI) = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100

Where:

  • Principal = Initial amount invested or borrowed
  • Rate = Interest rate per annum
  • Time = Time in years

Example Problem

Question: What will be the simple interest on ₹10,000 at 5% per annum for 3 years?

Solution: SI = (10000 × 5 × 3) / 100 = ₹1500

Tips & Tricks

  • Remember the formula SI = (P × R × T) / 100
  • Practice calculating percentages quickly to save time in exams.
  • For fractional years, adjust the time component accordingly.

Practice Questions

  1. Find the simple interest on ₹6000 at 4% per annum for 2.5 years.
  2. If the simple interest on ₹8000 for 3 years is ₹960, find the rate of interest.
  3. How long will it take to double the money at 6.25% simple interest per annum?
