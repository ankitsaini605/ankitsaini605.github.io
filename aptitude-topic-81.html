📘 Introduction to Clock Problems

Clock problems are common in aptitude tests and involve calculating angles between hands, mirror images, and gaining/losing time. EduIgnite helps you master these!

🧠 Key Concepts & Formulas

  • Angle between hour and minute hand:
    Angle = |(30×H) − (11/2)×M|
  • Hands coincide: Every 65 minutes.
  • Hands opposite (180° apart): Every 65 minutes / 2 = 32.5 minutes.
  • Mirror image: Mirror time = (11:60 - actual time)
  • Minute hand gains: 5.5° per minute on hour hand

⚡ Short Tricks

  • If asked about time between coincidences → use 65 minutes rule.
  • To calculate angle at any time → apply angle formula directly.
  • Use diagrams to visualize mirror/real time conversions.
  • In gain/loss questions → relative speed of minute and hour hands = 5.5°/min

📝 Examples

Q: Find the angle between hands at 3:20.
Sol: (30×3) − (11/2×20) = 90 − 110 = 20°

Q: At what time between 2 and 3 will the hands be opposite?
Sol: Use equation: (30×2) ± 180 = (11/2)×x → Solve → x = 43 5/11 min

🎯 100+ Practice MCQs on Clocks

  1. At what time between 4 and 5 will the hands be at right angle?
  2. How many times in a day do the hands coincide?
  3. If a clock gains 5 minutes every hour, how much will it gain in a day?
  4. Find the angle at 9:45.
  5. Mirror image of 3:25 is?
  6. How much angle does minute hand move in 12 minutes?
  7. Clock shows 2:00. What's the angle between the hands?

🧮 Solutions

  • Q1: Hands at right angle = 90° → Use formula → Solve
  • Q2: 22 times in 24 hours
  • Q3: 5×24 = 120 minutes
  • Q4: 30×9 - (11/2)×45 = 270 - 247.5 = 22.5°
  • Q5: Mirror image of 3:25 = 8:35

📌 Tips to Crack Clock Questions

  • Practice using diagrams.
  • Memorize standard angle values (30°, 90°, 180°).
  • Practice mirror image rules.
  • Focus on real-life situations like time loss/gain, digital vs analog, etc.
