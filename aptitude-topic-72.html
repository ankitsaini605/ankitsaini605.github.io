Topic 72: Time and Work

Master important concepts related to work efficiency, time taken by individuals or groups, and work-sharing ratios.

Practice Questions

  1. Q1. A can complete a task in 20 days, and B can do the same in 30 days. How many days will they take together?
  2. Q2. If A is twice as efficient as B, and together they finish a task in 12 days, how long would B take alone?
  3. Q3. A, B, and C can complete a work in 10, 20, and 30 days respectively. How long will it take if all work together?

Answers & Explanations

  • A1. 1/A + 1/B = 1/20 + 1/30 = (3 + 2)/60 = 5/60 = 1/12 → 12 days
  • A2. A = 2B → 1/A + 1/B = 1/12 → 1/2B + 1/B = 1/12 → (3/2B) = 1/12 → B = 18 days
  • A3. Total work = LCM(10, 20, 30) = 60 units → A = 6, B = 3, C = 2 → Combined = 11 units/day → Time = 60/11 ≈ 5.45 days

Expert Tip

Always convert individual work time into efficiencies and then sum them up when working together.
