🔍 Simple Interest (SI)

Simple Interest is the interest calculated only on the original principal amount for a certain period of time.

📐 Formula for Simple Interest:

SI = (P × R × T) / 100

  • P = Principal amount (initial money)
  • R = Rate of interest per annum (%)
  • T = Time period in years

Example 1:

Find the simple interest on ₹5000 at 5% per annum for 3 years.

Solution: SI = (5000 × 5 × 3)/100 = ₹750

🔍 Compound Interest (CI)

Compound Interest is the interest calculated on the principal amount plus the interest earned over previous periods.

📐 Formula for Compound Interest:

A = P (1 + R/100)^T where CI = A - P

  • A = Amount after time T
  • P = Principal amount
  • R = Rate of interest per annum (%)
  • T = Time period in years

Example 2:

Calculate the compound interest on ₹2000 at 10% per annum for 2 years.

Solution:
A = 2000 (1 + 10/100)^2 = 2000 × 1.1 × 1.1 = ₹2420
CI = 2420 - 2000 = ₹420

🔑 Key Points to Remember:

  • Simple Interest is linear and does not compound.
  • Compound Interest grows faster due to interest on interest.
  • When interest is compounded half-yearly or quarterly, adjust rate and time accordingly.

🎯 Practice Questions:

  1. Calculate the simple interest on ₹1500 at 8% per annum for 4 years.
  2. Find the amount and compound interest on ₹3000 at 6% per annum compounded annually for 3 years.
  3. If the compound interest on a sum for 2 years at 5% per annum is ₹520, find the principal.
