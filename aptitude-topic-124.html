Aptitude Topic 124 - Time and Work

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 124. In this section, you will explore Time and Work problems — one of the most important topics in aptitude exams.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • If A can do a piece of work in n days, then A's 1 day's work = 1/n.
  • If A is x times as good a workman as B, then ratio of work done by A and B = x : 1.
  • Work = Rate × Time.

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

A can complete a work in 12 days, and B can complete it in 16 days. In how many days will they complete the work together?

Solution:

A's 1 day work = 1/12
B's 1 day work = 1/16
Combined 1 day work = 1/12 + 1/16 = (4+3)/48 = 7/48
Time required = 48/7 ≈ 6.86 days

Question 2:

A and B together can complete a piece of work in 10 days. B alone can complete the work in 15 days. How long will A take to complete the work alone?

Solution:

A + B's 1 day work = 1/10
B's 1 day work = 1/15
A's 1 day work = 1/10 - 1/15 = (3-2)/30 = 1/30
Time required = 30 days

Question 3:

12 men can complete a work in 8 days. How many men are required to complete the same work in 6 days?

Solution:

Men × Days = Constant
12 × 8 = x × 6 → x = (12 × 8) / 6 = 16 men

Tips & Tricks

  • Convert work into rates to simplify calculations.
  • Use the LCM method when multiple workers are involved.
  • Understand inverse proportionality between men and time.
  • Practice a variety of questions to improve accuracy and speed.
