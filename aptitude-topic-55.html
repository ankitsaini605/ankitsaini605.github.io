Aptitude Topic 55: Advanced Time and Work Problems

Explore challenging time and work problems designed to sharpen your problem-solving skills for exams and interviews.

Problem 1:

A is twice as efficient as B and together they can finish a piece of work in 12 days. How long will B take to complete the work alone?
Solution:
Let B's 1 day work = x
Then A's 1 day work = 2x
Together, 1 day work = x + 2x = 3x
Given they finish work in 12 days, so 1 day work = 1/12
Therefore, 3x = 1/12 → x = 1/36
B alone will take = 1 / (1/36) = 36 days
Answer: B will take 36 days

Problem 2:

Pipe A can fill a tank in 6 hours, pipe B can fill it in 8 hours, and pipe C can empty it in 12 hours. If all pipes are opened together, how long will it take to fill the tank?
Solution:
A's 1 hour work = 1/6
B's 1 hour work = 1/8
C's 1 hour work (emptying) = -1/12
Combined 1 hour work = 1/6 + 1/8 - 1/12 = (4 + 3 - 2)/24 = 5/24
Time to fill tank = 1 / (5/24) = 24/5 = 4.8 hours = 4 hours 48 minutes
Answer: 4 hours 48 minutes

Problem 3:

A and B together can do a work in 10 days. A alone can do it in 15 days. How long will B alone take to do the work?
Solution:
A's 1 day work = 1/15
(A + B)'s 1 day work = 1/10
B's 1 day work = 1/10 - 1/15 = (3 - 2)/30 = 1/30
B alone will take = 1 / (1/30) = 30 days
Answer: B will take 30 days
