Aptitude Topic 14 - HCF and LCM

Highest Common Factor (HCF) and Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) are important concepts in quantitative aptitude tests. Understanding these concepts can help in solving many problems related to time, work, speed, and more.

Basic Definitions

  • HCF: The greatest number that divides each of the given numbers exactly.
  • LCM: The smallest number which is exactly divisible by each of the given numbers.

Important Formulas

  • Product of two numbers = HCF × LCM
  • HCF of fractions = HCF of numerators / LCM of denominators
  • LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators / HCF of denominators

Example Problem

Question: Find the HCF and LCM of 72 and 120.

Solution:

Prime factors of 72 = 2³ × 3²
Prime factors of 120 = 2³ × 3 × 5
HCF = 2³ × 3 = 24
LCM = 2³ × 3² × 5 = 360

Tips & Tricks

  • Break numbers into prime factors for easier calculation.
  • Remember the product of numbers = HCF × LCM formula for cross-verification.
  • Practice with multiple types of questions to gain speed and accuracy.

Practice Questions

  1. Find the HCF and LCM of 56 and 98.
  2. If the LCM of two numbers is 180 and their HCF is 6, find the product of the numbers.
  3. Find the least number which is exactly divisible by 15, 20, and 25.
