Aptitude Topic 111 - Problems on Numbers

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 111. This section covers Problems on Numbers — one of the most important topics for all competitive exams.

Concepts Covered

  • Types of Numbers (Natural, Whole, Integers, Rational, Irrational)
  • Number Properties (Even, Odd, Prime, Composite)
  • Divisibility Rules
  • LCM and HCF
  • Unit Digit, Remainder Concepts

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

The sum of three consecutive even numbers is 48. Find the numbers.

Solution:

Let the numbers be x, x+2, x+4.

x + (x+2) + (x+4) = 48

3x + 6 = 48 → 3x = 42 → x = 14.

Numbers are: 14, 16, 18.

Question 2:

Find the least number which when divided by 5, 6, 8 leaves a remainder 3 in each case.

Solution:

LCM of 5, 6, 8 = 120.

Required number = 120k + 3.

Smallest such number = 120(1) + 3 = 123.

Question 3:

What is the unit digit of 7173?

Solution:

Unit digit of powers of 7 repeats every 4: 7, 9, 3, 1.

173 mod 4 = 1 → 1st term → unit digit = 7.

Expert Tips

  • Learn divisibility rules to quickly filter number choices.
  • Use cyclicity for unit digit problems.
  • Know LCM & HCF concepts well — very useful in exam questions.
  • Be comfortable with types of numbers and their properties.
