Introduction

Simple Interest (SI) is the interest calculated only on the principal amount, or on that portion of the principal amount which remains unpaid.

Key Concepts

  • Principal (P): The initial amount of money invested or loaned.
  • Rate (R): The percentage of interest charged or earned annually.
  • Time (T): The time period for which the money is invested or borrowed, usually in years.
  • Simple Interest (SI): The interest earned or paid on the principal only.

Formulas

  • Simple Interest: SI = (P × R × T) / 100
  • Amount: A = P + SI
  • Principal: P = (SI × 100) / (R × T)
  • Rate: R = (SI × 100) / (P × T)
  • Time: T = (SI × 100) / (P × R)

Practice Problems

Problem 1

If ₹10,000 is invested at a simple interest rate of 5% per annum for 3 years, what is the total interest earned?

SI = (P × R × T) / 100 = (10000 × 5 × 3) / 100 = ₹1500

Problem 2

A sum of ₹12,000 amounts to ₹13,800 in 3 years at simple interest. Find the rate of interest.

SI = 13800 - 12000 = ₹1800
Using SI formula: R = (SI × 100) / (P × T) = (1800 × 100) / (12000 × 3) = 5%

Tips & Tricks

  • Convert time to years if given in months or days.
  • Always remember to convert rate to percentage if given in decimal.
  • Use the formula carefully to avoid common mistakes.
