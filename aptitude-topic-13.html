Aptitude Topic 13 - Simple Interest and Compound Interest

This topic covers the fundamental concepts of Simple Interest (SI) and Compound Interest (CI), crucial for banking and competitive exams.

Simple Interest (SI)

Simple Interest is calculated only on the principal amount.

  • Formula: SI = (P × R × T) / 100
  • P = Principal amount
  • R = Rate of interest per annum (%)
  • T = Time in years

Compound Interest (CI)

Compound Interest is calculated on the principal plus accumulated interest.

  • Formula for CI: CI = P(1 + R/100)T - P
  • Where the interest is compounded annually.
  • For other compounding periods: CI = P(1 + R/(100×n))nT - P, where n = number of times interest compounded per year.

Practice Questions

Question 1:

If the simple interest on a sum for 3 years at 5% per annum is ₹750, find the principal.

Solution:

SI = (P × R × T)/100 = 750
P = (750 × 100) / (5 × 3) = ₹5000

Question 2:

Find the compound interest on ₹10,000 for 2 years at 10% per annum compounded annually.

Solution:

CI = 10000(1 + 10/100)^2 - 10000 = 10000 × (1.1)^2 - 10000 = 10000 × 1.21 - 10000 = ₹2100

Tips & Tricks

  • Always convert percentage to decimal when using formulas.
  • Use the compound interest formula carefully for different compounding periods.
  • Remember that CI is always greater than or equal to SI for the same principal and rate.
