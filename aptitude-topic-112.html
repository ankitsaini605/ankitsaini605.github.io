Aptitude Topic 112 - Problems on Ages

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 112. This section will help you master Problems on Ages — a common topic in various aptitude and competitive exams.

Concepts Covered

  • Present, Past, and Future Ages
  • Age Difference Problems
  • Ratio of Ages
  • Age After 'n' Years and 'n' Years Ago
  • Linear Equations in Age Problems

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

The sum of the ages of A and B is 50 years. After 5 years, the ratio of their ages will be 6:5. Find their present ages.

Solution:

Let A's age = x, B's age = 50 - x.

(x + 5) / (50 - x + 5) = 6 / 5.

Cross-multiply and solve: 5(x + 5) = 6(55 - x).

5x + 25 = 330 - 6x → 11x = 305 → x = 27.73 ≈ 28 years.

B's age = 50 - 28 = 22 years.

Question 2:

Father’s age is three times the son’s age. After 10 years, father’s age will be twice that of his son. Find their present ages.

Solution:

Let son's age = x → father's age = 3x.

After 10 years: (3x + 10) = 2(x + 10).

3x + 10 = 2x + 20 → x = 10 years.

Father’s age = 3 × 10 = 30 years.

Question 3:

The present ages of A and B are in the ratio 4:5. After 6 years, the ratio of their ages will be 5:6. Find their present ages.

Solution:

Let present ages be 4x and 5x.

(4x + 6) / (5x + 6) = 5 / 6.

Cross-multiply and solve: 6(4x + 6) = 5(5x + 6).

24x + 36 = 25x + 30 → x = 6 years.

A's age = 24 years, B's age = 30 years.

Expert Tips

  • Always translate word problems into algebraic equations.
  • Pay close attention to the wording: "after n years," "n years ago."
  • Draw a table if necessary to compare present and future/past ages.
  • Practice with a variety of ratio-based and difference-based age problems.
