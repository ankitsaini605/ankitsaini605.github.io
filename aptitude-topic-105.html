Aptitude Topic 105 - Average Problems

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 105. This topic focuses on understanding and applying the concept of averages. Questions on averages frequently appear in competitive exams.

Concepts Covered

  • Definition of Average
  • Average of Numbers
  • Weighted Average
  • Applications of Average in Real-life Problems

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

The average of five numbers is 20. If one number is removed, the average becomes 18. Find the number removed.

Solution:

Total of five numbers = 5 * 20 = 100.

Total of remaining four numbers = 4 * 18 = 72.

Number removed = 100 - 72 = 28.

Question 2:

The average marks obtained by 30 students is 45. The average marks of 10 students is 50. Find the average marks of the remaining 20 students.

Solution:

Total marks of 30 students = 30 * 45 = 1350.

Total marks of 10 students = 10 * 50 = 500.

Total marks of remaining 20 students = 1350 - 500 = 850.

Average of 20 students = 850 / 20 = 42.5.

Question 3:

The average age of a group of 6 persons is 40 years. A new person joins the group, increasing the average age to 42 years. Find the age of the new person.

Solution:

Total age of 6 persons = 6 * 40 = 240 years.

Total age of 7 persons = 7 * 42 = 294 years.

Age of new person = 294 - 240 = 54 years.

Expert Tips

  • Average = (Sum of Observations) / (Number of Observations).
  • Use the weighted average formula for combined groups.
  • In exam problems, calculate total first to avoid mistakes.
  • Be careful with changing group size when someone joins or leaves.
