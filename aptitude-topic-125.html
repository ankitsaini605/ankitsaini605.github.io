Aptitude Topic 125 - Pipes and Cisterns

Welcome to Aptitude Topic 125. In this section, we will study Pipes and Cisterns problems which are commonly asked in many competitive exams.

Key Concepts & Formulas

  • If a pipe can fill a tank in n hours, then the part filled in 1 hour = 1/n.
  • If a pipe can empty a tank in n hours, then the part emptied in 1 hour = 1/n.
  • If one pipe fills and another empties, the net part filled/emptied in 1 hour = difference of individual rates.

Practice Questions with Solutions

Question 1:

A pipe can fill a tank in 12 hours. Another pipe can empty the tank in 16 hours. If both are opened together, how long will it take to fill the tank?

Solution:

Filling rate = 1/12
Emptying rate = -1/16
Net rate = 1/12 - 1/16 = (4-3)/48 = 1/48
Time required = 48 hours

Question 2:

Two pipes can fill a tank in 15 minutes and 20 minutes respectively. If both pipes are opened together, how long will it take to fill the tank?

Solution:

1st pipe's rate = 1/15
2nd pipe's rate = 1/20
Combined rate = (4+3)/60 = 7/60
Time required = 60/7 ≈ 8.57 minutes

Question 3:

A cistern is normally filled in 8 hours. But due to a leak, it takes 10 hours to fill. In how many hours will the leak empty the cistern?

Solution:

Filling rate = 1/8
Net rate = 1/10
Leak rate = 1/8 - 1/10 = (5-4)/40 = 1/40
Time required to empty = 40 hours

Tips & Tricks

  • Convert all times into rates to simplify calculations.
  • Remember: filling = positive rate, emptying = negative rate.
  • Practice a mix of both filling and leak problems.
