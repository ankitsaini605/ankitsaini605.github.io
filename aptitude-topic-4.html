What is Percentage?

Percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It is widely used in daily life as well as in competitive exams.

Key Concepts

  • Percentage Formula: (Part / Whole) × 100%
  • Increase/Decrease Percentage: ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100%
  • Successive Percentage Change: Use multiplication of respective multipliers.

Important Formulas

  • Percentage = (Value × 100) / Total
  • Value = (Percentage × Total) / 100
  • Successive Change = (1 + p/100)(1 + q/100) - 1, where p and q are percentages

Practice Problems

Problem 1

If 40% of a number is 80, what is the number?

Let the number be x.

40% of x = 80

(40/100) × x = 80

x = (80 × 100) / 40 = 200

Problem 2

The price of an article is increased by 20% and then decreased by 10%. What is the net percentage change in price?

Net change = (1 + 20/100)(1 - 10/100) - 1

= (1.2)(0.9) - 1 = 1.08 - 1 = 0.08 = 8%

Therefore, price increases by 8% overall.

Tips & Tricks

  • Convert percentage problems into fractions or decimals for easier calculation.
  • For successive changes, multiply multipliers instead of adding percentages directly.
  • Practice word problems to understand real-life applications.
