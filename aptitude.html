Percentages

Key Formulas

Percentage = (Value / Total Value) × 100

Value = (Percentage × Total Value) / 100

Percentage Change = ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100

Successive Percentage Change: If there are two successive percentage changes of x% and y%, the net percentage change = (1 + x/100) × (1 + y/100) - 1

Example 1: Basic Percentage Calculation Easy

Question: What is 25% of 80?

Solution:
  • We need to find 25% of 80.
  • Using the formula: Value = (Percentage × Total Value) / 100
  • Value = (25 × 80) / 100
  • Value = 2000 / 100 = 20
  • Therefore, 25% of 80 is 20.

Example 2: Percentage Change Medium

Question: The price of a laptop was ₹50,000 last year. This year, the price increased to ₹56,000. What is the percentage increase in the price?

Solution:
  • Original price = ₹50,000
  • New price = ₹56,000
  • Using the formula: Percentage Change = ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) × 100
  • Percentage Change = ((56,000 - 50,000) / 50,000) × 100
  • Percentage Change = (6,000 / 50,000) × 100
  • Percentage Change = 0.12 × 100 = 12%
  • Therefore, the percentage increase in the price is 12%.

Example 3: Successive Percentage Changes Hard

Question: The price of a product increases by 20% and then decreases by 10%. What is the net percentage change in the price?

Solution:
  • First percentage change (increase) = 20%
  • Second percentage change (decrease) = -10%
  • Using the formula for successive percentage changes: Net percentage change = (1 + x/100) × (1 + y/100) - 1
  • Net percentage change = (1 + 20/100) × (1 + (-10)/100) - 1
  • Net percentage change = (1 + 0.2) × (1 - 0.1) - 1
  • Net percentage change = 1.2 × 0.9 - 1
  • Net percentage change = 1.08 - 1 = 0.08
  • Net percentage change = 0.08 × 100 = 8%
  • Therefore, the net percentage change is an increase of 8%.

Profit & Loss

Key Formulas

Profit = Selling Price (SP) - Cost Price (CP)

Loss = Cost Price (CP) - Selling Price (SP)

Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100

Loss Percentage = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100

Selling Price = Cost Price × (1 + Profit% / 100)

Selling Price = Cost Price × (1 - Loss% / 100)

Marked Price = Selling Price / (1 - Discount% / 100)

Example 1: Basic Profit Calculation Easy

Question: A shopkeeper buys a watch for ₹1,200 and sells it for ₹1,500. What is the profit percentage?

Solution:
  • Cost Price (CP) = ₹1,200
  • Selling Price (SP) = ₹1,500
  • Profit = SP - CP = 1,500 - 1,200 = ₹300
  • Using the formula: Profit Percentage = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100
  • Profit Percentage = (300 / 1,200) × 100
  • Profit Percentage = 0.25 × 100 = 25%
  • Therefore, the profit percentage is 25%.

Example 2: Marked Price and Discount Medium

Question: A shopkeeper marks his goods 40% above the cost price and gives a discount of 20% on the marked price. What is his profit percentage?

Solution:
  • Let's assume the cost price (CP) is ₹100 for easier calculation.
  • Marked Price (MP) = CP + 40% of CP = 100 + 40 = ₹140
  • Discount = 20% of MP = 20% of 140 = ₹28
  • Selling Price (SP) = MP - Discount = 140 - 28 = ₹112
  • Profit = SP - CP = 112 - 100 = ₹12
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) × 100 = (12 / 100) × 100 = 12%
  • Therefore, the profit percentage is 12%.

Simple & Compound Interest

Key Formulas

Simple Interest (SI) = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100

Amount after Simple Interest = Principal + Simple Interest

Compound Interest (CI) = Principal × [(1 + Rate/100)^Time - 1]

Amount after Compound Interest = Principal × (1 + Rate/100)^Time

For half-yearly compounding: Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/200)^(2×Time)

For quarterly compounding: Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/400)^(4×Time)

Example 1: Simple Interest Easy

Question: Calculate the simple interest on ₹5,000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.

Solution:
  • Principal (P) = ₹5,000
  • Rate (R) = 8% per annum
  • Time (T) = 3 years
  • Using the formula: Simple Interest (SI) = (P × R × T) / 100
  • SI = (5,000 × 8 × 3) / 100
  • SI = 120,000 / 100 = ₹1,200
  • Therefore, the simple interest is ₹1,200.
  • Amount after 3 years = Principal + SI = 5,000 + 1,200 = ₹6,200

Example 2: Compound Interest Medium

Question: Calculate the compound interest on ₹10,000 at 10% per annum for 2 years, compounded annually.

Solution:
  • Principal (P) = ₹10,000
  • Rate (R) = 10% per annum
  • Time (T) = 2 years
  • Using the formula: Amount = P × (1 + R/100)^T
  • Amount = 10,000 × (1 + 10/100)^2
  • Amount = 10,000 × (1.1)^2
  • Amount = 10,000 × 1.21 = ₹12,100
  • Compound Interest = Amount - Principal = 12,100 - 10,000 = ₹2,100
  • Therefore, the compound interest is ₹2,100.

Tips for Mastering Aptitude

  • Practice Regularly

    Consistent practice is the key to improving your aptitude skills. Set aside at least 30 minutes daily for solving problems.

  • Master Mental Math

    Develop your mental calculation skills to save time during exams. Learn shortcuts for multiplication, division, and percentage calculations.

  • Time Management

    Practice solving problems within time constraints. Use a timer during your practice sessions to improve your speed.

  • Learn the Formulas

    Memorize all the important formulas and understand when and how to apply them. Create a formula sheet for quick reference.

    Review your incorrect answers to understand where you went wrong. Learning from mistakes is crucial for improvement.
