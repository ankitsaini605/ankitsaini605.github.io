Aptitude Topic 59: Profit and Loss Problems

Explore key Profit and Loss questions with clear, stepwise solutions to strengthen your concepts and perform well in exams.

Problem 1:

A trader buys an article for ₹500 and sells it for ₹600. Find his profit percentage.
Solution:
Cost Price (CP) = ₹500
Selling Price (SP) = ₹600
Profit = SP - CP = 600 - 500 = ₹100
Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100 = (100 / 500) × 100 = 20%
Answer: 20% profit

Problem 2:

A shopkeeper sells an article at 10% loss. If the selling price is ₹450, find the cost price.
Solution:
Let Cost Price = ₹x
Loss = 10% of CP = 0.10 × x
Selling Price (SP) = CP - Loss = x - 0.10x = 0.90x
Given SP = ₹450
0.90x = 450 → x = 450 / 0.90 = ₹500
Answer: Cost Price = ₹500

Problem 3:

An article is sold for ₹720 after allowing a discount of 10% on the marked price. If the profit is 20%, find the marked price.
Solution:
Let Marked Price = ₹M
Selling Price (SP) = ₹720
Discount = 10% of M = 0.10M
SP = Marked Price - Discount = M - 0.10M = 0.90M → 0.90M = 720 → M = 720 / 0.90 = ₹800
Profit = 20% means SP = 120% of Cost Price (CP)
So, CP = SP / 1.20 = 720 / 1.20 = ₹600
Answer: Marked Price = ₹800, Cost Price = ₹600
