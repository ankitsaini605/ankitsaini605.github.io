🔍 Concept Overview

Time & Work problems involve understanding how long multiple agents (people, machines) take to finish a job together or alone. Very common in competitive exams.

Key Formula:
  • Work done per day = 1 / days to finish job
  • If A does work in a days and B in b days, together = 1/a + 1/b

⚡ Tricks & Shortcuts

  • If multiple people working at same rate, multiply accordingly.
  • For pipe-in & pipe-out problems, treat emptying as negative work.

🎯 100+ MCQs

  1. A can do a job in 8 days, B in 12 days. How long working together?
  2. A takes twice as long as B to do same job. Together they take 15 days. How long B alone?
  3. Pipe A fills in 10 hrs, pipe B in 15 hrs, pipe C empties in 20 hrs. All three open: time to fill?

✅ Answer Key

  • 1. 8 & 12 → Together = 8*12/20 = 4.8 days
  • 2. Let B = x → A = 2x → eqn: 1/2x+1/x=1/15 → x=10 days → B=10
  • 3. Rate = 1/10+1/15–1/20 = (6+4–3)/60 = 7/60 → 60/7 ≈ 8.57 hrs

📌 Practice Tips

  • Set up equations slowly, label clearly.
  • Convert all units (hours/days) consistently.
  • Use calculator above to check answers quickly.
